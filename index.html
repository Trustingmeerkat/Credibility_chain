Credibility Chain

A structured reasoning network with self-derived reliability.

We have accepted that information cannot be trusted on its own merits. We treat this as a permanent condition. It is not. It is an infrastructure problem.

This paper proposes a system in which any entity — human or machine — can query a piece of reasoning and receive a measure of how much it deserves to be believed. Not based on who said it. Based on its logical structure and track record.

The core whitepaper is four pages. It lays out the argument, the mechanism, and the reliability model. The remaining pages decompose the paper’s own claims into structured arguments and express them in machine-readable form.

The document practices what it preaches.
